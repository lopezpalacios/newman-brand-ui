01 · The Logo

Isotype + wordmark.

Five descending diagonal bars form an abstract “N” that reads as solar rays and momentum — the isotype, which always keeps its gradient. The wordmark is a bold neo-grotesque that flips to suit the surface. The isotype's navy end can camouflage on dark; on dark surfaces use the reversed all-white lockup, never the gradient.

Primary — navy wordmark + gradient isotype, on light surfaces.

Reversed — all white, on navy / photography. Use this in the nav and footer.

Isotype

Standalone for avatars, favicons, app icons. Min 16px.

Clear space & min size

Clear space = one isotype-bar height on all sides. Min lockup 120px wide.

Construction & tests

Built on a 10×10 unit grid; all five bars share one diagonal axis (~42° from horizontal). The mark is constructed, not drawn — reproducible at any size.

Monochrome & inverted

Scale test — 16 → 48px

Reads at 16px; survives one ink, black-on-white, and white-on-black. A reductive silhouette — the test every iconic mark passes.

Recognition & the app mark

The mark owns a tile — reversed white isotype in a rounded square (radius ≈ 25% of width). This is the form recognized at a glance on a home screen or avatar. One shape, one colour, instantly Newman. Hover a tile — the mark lifts. A small, deliberate moment of delight; correctness alone never made anyone love a brand.

One-glance reading

The five descending bars resolve two ways at once: an abstract N for Newman, and a fan of solar rays for energy. A single silhouette carrying dual meaning — read in well under a second, no language required, the same on any continent.

Recognition test: cover the wordmark. The isotype alone still says the brand — the bar of a mark that earns standalone use.

Logo in motion

The signature animation: the five bars rise and assemble in sequence, longest first — a gesture of upward momentum and energy coming online. Use it for splash, intro, and loading moments.

Build only (never disassemble on screen), ~0.6s with a 90ms stagger, ease-out. Honors prefers-reduced-motion — the mark simply appears, fully formed.

Misuse — never do this

✕ Don't rotate or tilt
✕ Don't stretch or distort
✕ Don't recolour the mark
✕ Don't put the gradient isotype on dark
✕ Don't add shadows or effects
Newman
✕ Don't retype the wordmark

The hidden reading

Every bar rides the same rising diagonal. Extend them and the mark resolves into a fan of dawn light — energy coming online at sunrise. It's the same gesture the logo animation performs, frozen. The eye feels the momentum before it names it.

Like FedEx's arrow, the ingenuity is quiet: an abstract N, a burst of solar rays, and an upward dawn — three readings in one silhouette, none of them shouted.

03 · Typography

Space Grotesk + Inter.

Space Grotesk carries display, headings and labels; the signature is weight 300 at large sizes — architectural, never shouted. Inter handles body. Never Space Grotesk below 12px; never tint body text magenta.

Grid & measure

Everything sits on a 12-column grid — 1200px max width, 24px gutters, centered. Two typefaces, four weights, one body measure (≤64ch). Nothing is placed by eye; the grid removes the arbitrary. The discipline is the design.

06 · Do & Don't

Protect the system.

Do

  • Lead with canvas / surface / navy; let solid accent be the rare highlight.
  • Set display headlines in Space Grotesk 300 at large sizes.
  • Prefix every section label with the 4px magenta square.
  • Keep rounding generous — 100px pills, 64px media, 24px cards.
  • Use the reversed white logo on dark surfaces.

Don't

  • Don't add chrome gradients — gradient belongs only in the logo isotype.
  • Don't tint body text magenta — it fails contrast.
  • Don't use bold/semibold for display headlines.
  • Don't add drop shadows — stay flat and tonal.
  • Don't put the gradient isotype on navy — it camouflages.

07 · Applications

Type as image, at any scale.

The identity holds from a 16px favicon to a building façade. Lead with the mark and big tracked-caps display — at scale, the type is the image.
